Abbas Acar

Postdoctoral Associate

Florida International University

I completed my PhD in the Cyber-Physical Systems Security (CSL) lab under the supervision of Dr. Selcuk Uluagac at Florida International University (FIU) in 2020. Currently, I am working as Postdoctoral Associate in the same lab within the Knight Foundation School of Computing and Information Science (KFSCIS). I received my BSc from Electrical and Electronics Engineering at Middle East Technical University, Turkey in 2015 with a minor in Mathematics.

I have been actively conducting system security research with a particular focus on practical and impactful perspectives. In my research, the fundamental building blocks are cryptography and machine learning. I leverage state-of-the-art cryptographic constructions such as homomorphic encryption and differential privacy, to create privacy-aware and secure frameworks, while also harnessing machine learning for the development of robust intrusion detection systems.
